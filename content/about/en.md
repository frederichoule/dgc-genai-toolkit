The Low-Carbon Generative AI Toolkit was developed to help creators better understand and reduce the environmental footprint associated with the use of generative AI. Ultimately, this would encourage a responsible and ethical use of AI.

## Why this project exists

The use of generative AI relies on energy-intensive infrastructure, including data centers and large-scale computing systems. While these technologies enable new forms of creativity and efficiency, they also contribute to growing energy consumption and carbon emissions.

This project responds to the current lack of practical tools by helping creators assess the environmental impact of AI in a clear and accessible way, making it more tangible and easier to integrate into everyday decision-making.

## DGC's context

DGC Green is the Directors Guild of Canada's sustainability initiative, empowering members with practical tools, guidance, and resources to reduce the environmental impact of screen production. From carbon-conscious best practices to industry collaboration, DGC Green helps Guild members make informed creative and production decisions that support a more sustainable screen industry.

[www.dgcgreen.ca](http://www.dgcgreen.ca)

## About the DGC

**The Directors Guild of Canada** (DGC) is a national labour organization representing creative and production professionals across the screen industry, including directing, design, production and editing. Founded in 1962, the Guild supports members' employment and creative opportunities while advancing policy and professional leadership in Canada and internationally.

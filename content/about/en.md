## Overview
The Low-Carbon Generative AI Toolkit was developed to help creators better understand the environmental footprint associated with the use of generative AI. Our hope is that, by sharing knowledge and best practice, we will encourage a more responsible and ethical use of AI across the screen sector.

## Why this project exists
The use of generative AI relies on energy-intensive infrastructure, including data centers and large-scale computing systems. While these technologies enable new forms of creativity and efficiency, they also contribute to growing energy consumption and carbon emissions.

This project responds to the current lack of standardised guidance for responsible AI use, by empowering creators to independently understand its environmental impact. We aim to do this in a clear and accessible way, making it easy to integrate best practice into everyday decision-making.


## About the methodology

The estimates presented in this project are based on Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) approaches and draw on emerging international standards, including recent work from the International Telecommunication Union (ITU).

Given the complexity of AI systems, the results should be understood as informed estimates rather than precise measurements. The objective is to provide a consistent and transparent framework for understanding impact.
If you’d like to read more about the methodology, check out the [Calculator page](/en/calculator). 

## Project context

This DGC Green initiative was developed by and for creators working in the screen industry, in response to the increasing integration of generative AI into creative processes.

It welcomes collaboration with industry partners and is informed by ongoing work at the intersection of technology, sustainability, and audiovisual production.

## About the DGC
The Low-Carbon GenAI Toolkit is an initiative of the [Directors Guild of Canada](http://www.dgc.ca) (DGC), a national labour organization representing creative and production professionals across the screen industry, including directing, design, production, and editing. Founded in 1962, the Guild supports members’ employment and creative opportunities while advancing policy leadership in Canada and internationally.

This project is delivered through [DGC Green](http://www.dgcgreen.ca), the Guild’s sustainability initiative, which develops practical tools, research, and guidance to help reduce the environmental impact of screen production and support more sustainable creative practices. From carbon-conscious best practices to industry collaboration, DGC Green helps Guild members make informed creative and production decisions that support a more sustainable screen industry.

## Made by Decarbonade
Montreal-based digital sustainability studio [Decarbonade](https://www.decarbonade.co/) has supported DGC’s efforts by building calculation models to estimate the environmental impact of GenAI, writing best practice content and developing the website in line with low-carbon and digital sustainability best practices.

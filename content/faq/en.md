## Understanding Generative AI impact

### What do you mean by “the environmental impact of generative AI (GenAI)”?

Generative AI (such as Open AI’s ChatGPT or Anthropic’s Claude) requires significant computing power to process prompts and generate outputs. This computing relies on data centers that require a lot of energy and water, resulting in greenhouse gas emissions and water scarcity in some regions, amongst other rising issues. The impact varies widely depending on the type of task, model, and infrastructure used. 

Looking beyond the data centers, end-users and network apparatus (antennas, cables, satellites) can carry significant impact too. For example, through energy consumption, resource extraction, manufacturing and transportation, making the environmental impact of AI a far-reaching and complex systems challenge.


### Why does GenAI consume so much energy?

AI models process vast amounts of data through complex calculations. Tasks like generating images or videos require far more computing power than simpler tasks such as text generation, leading to higher energy use. 

### Is using GenAI worse than traditional digital tools?

Almost always. Certain tasks — especially image and video generation and using reasoning models — can have a significantly higher footprint than standard digital activities such as using a computer or browsing the internet. It is also important to note that non-AI production tasks consume energy and should be accounted for accordingly.

### Why should creators care about this?

Because AI is becoming part of everyday creative workflows. Understanding its impact allows creators to make informed choices without compromising their work, and to align with broader sustainability goals in the industry.

### Isn't this just a data center problem?

Infrastructure matters, but both network and end-users play a role (total AI system footprint and energy consumption). AI giants actively drive adoption by embedding AI into everyday tools and incentives, increasing demand and, in turn, accelerating infrastructure expansion.

### Should I stop using generative AI altogether?

No. The goal is not to eliminate use, but to use it more consciously and efficiently, reserving it for cases where it is essential or when no viable alternative exists.


## How This Toolkit Applies to Your Work

### Who is this toolkit for?

It is primarily intended for DGC Members who consider using generative AI, but is well-suited to all creators and professionals in the screen industry. It can be used by anyone interested in understanding the environmental impact of generative AI.

### How does this relate to broader sustainability efforts?

This toolkit complements existing initiatives in the screen industry focused on reducing production emissions, by addressing digital and AI-related impacts. It is meant to evolve as we collect data on the calculator’s use.


### Isn't the AI impact negligible compared to film production's other emissions?

AI’s impact is still emerging but growing rapidly. Addressing it early helps avoid scaling unsustainable practices.


### Can the toolkit be used at the production level?

Yes. The toolkit can inform discussions on workflows, tool selection, and digital practices across teams.

## GenAI Carbon Calculator 

### What does the GenAI carbon calculator do?
The calculator estimates the energy use and carbon footprint of common generative AI tasks, helping you understand the impact of your own individual usage. The calculator output is limited to estimated CO2e emissions, according to available data.


### Are the results exact?
No. The results are estimates based on current research and average estimates. They are designed to provide order-of-magnitude insights, not precise measurements. Today, only a few AI companies disclose information both about the training and energy output associated with the usage of their tools.


### What information do I need to use the calculator?
You simply select the type of task (text, image, video, or audio) and your usage patterns. The tool translates this into an estimated environmental impact. 

### Why does the calculator focus on individual usage?
Because small, repeated actions scale. When multiplied across thousands of users and workflows, individual choices have a meaningful cumulative impact. The calculator currently does not have the ability to measure AI use across the different phases of a production. 

### Does using AI more efficiently really make a difference?
Yes. Reducing iterations, improving prompts, and selecting appropriate tools can significantly lower energy use over time. Explore [best practices](/en/guide) and discover what you can do.


### Will the calculator data be updated?
Yes. As AI technologies and measurement methods evolve, the toolkit will be updated to reflect the most accurate information available. The most recent update to the calculator dataset was made on March 01, 2026.
